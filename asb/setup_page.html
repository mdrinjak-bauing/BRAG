Step 2 — set up Ollama

Install Ollama and download two models

  1. Install Ollama (free): ollama.com → Download → install. It runs quietly in the background (menu bar / tray icon).
  2. Download the models — open the Terminal (Mac: Spotlight → “Terminal”; Windows: Start → “cmd”) and run these two lines, one after the other (each downloads a few GB):
    ollama pull nomic-embed-text
    ollama pull llama3.1

    16 GB memory → stay with llama3.1. Stronger machine? ollama pull qwen2.5:14b gives better analysis quality.

How your second brain works

📚 Your library

vault/sources/

Drop documents here — they are indexed automatically. Claude searches them and cites with page numbers. You never edit these; you just collect.

📓 Your notebook

vault/wiki/ & notes/

Your own thinking. Deliberately not in the search index — otherwise you'd one day cite your own notes as “evidence” without noticing. Claude can still read & write it via the optional Obsidian connection.

The loop that makes it a second brain

  1. New paper? Drop it into sources/ — then ask: “What does this change about my topic?”
  2. Develop ideas with Claude, grounded in your sources — then keep the result: “Write this up as a concept note in wiki/.”
  3. Days later, in a fresh chat: “Open my concept note and let's continue.” The new conversation starts where the old one ended.

Chats forget — your vault doesn't. Knowledge accumulates in your files, not in the chat history.
